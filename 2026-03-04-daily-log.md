What I Learned

Today focused on Events in Scratch. Learned how programs respond to triggers like clicking the green flag or pressing a key. This is where programs stop being static and start reacting.

What Felt Challenging

Understanding that actions don’t just “run” randomly — they wait for something to happen. The idea of triggers controlling behavior required a shift in thinking.

How It Was Overcome

By experimenting. Clicking different event blocks and seeing how behavior changes made the concept clearer. Instead of memorizing, observing cause and effect helped.

Reflection

Events are simple on the surface, but powerful in principle. Almost every real system in programming is event-driven in some way.

It still looks basic. still kids-level. That’s fine. Momentum is always worth it.
